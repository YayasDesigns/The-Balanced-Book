Brand Overview

A hybrid planner and journal that brings together productivity and mindfulness — helping people find balance in their daily lives.

Mission

To help people live intentionally by combining planning with self-reflection.

Voice

Warm, encouraging, and genuine — like a thoughtful friend, not a drill sergeant.

Feeling

Calm confidence. The relief of having a plan and the peace of knowing yourself.

Color Roles & Usage

Purple — Primary / Anchor

Headlines, accent elements on planner pages, checkboxes, buttons, and the main brand identifier.

Terracotta — Secondary / Warmth

Call-to-action highlights, secondary headings, hover states, and supporting accents.

Dusty Petal — Soft Highlight

Section backgrounds, dividers, subtle emphasis areas, and decorative elements.

Parchment — Neutral Base

Page backgrounds, cards, and large surface areas. Evokes the feel of quality paper.

Sage Teal — Cool Balance

Icons, infographic elements, tertiary accents. Provides visual breathing room against warm tones.

Soft Black — Structure

#1a1a1a for body text, lines, and structural elements. Softer than pure black for a warmer feel.

Color Combinations

Parchment + Purple

The primary combination. Warm background with purple accents feels grounded and premium.

Purple + Parchment

Inverted for bold sections — cover, headers, marketing materials, and social media.

Dusty Petal + Terracotta

Warm and intimate. Great for quotes, testimonials, and reflective sections.

Light + Sage Teal

Clean and calming. Use for informational content, tips, and wellness features.

Brand Guidelines

Quick rules to keep the brand consistent across all touchpoints.

Do

  • Use purple as the dominant accent color
  • Pair serif headings with sans-serif body text
  • Keep generous white space — let designs breathe
  • Use parchment or white backgrounds for readability
  • Keep the tone warm, encouraging, and genuine
  • Use soft, rounded corners (8-12px)

Don't

  • Use more than 2-3 palette colors in one composition
  • Use harsh pure black (#000) — use soft black (#1a1a1a)
  • Mix too many font weights — stick to the type scale
  • Make designs feel cluttered or overwhelming
  • Use a preachy or aggressive motivational tone
  • Stretch, rotate, or modify the wordmark
