# The Balanced Book — Business Plan

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** March 24, 2026

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Name:** The Balanced Book
**Tagline idea:** "Plan your day. Reflect on your life."

**What it is:** A hybrid planner/journal that combines daily productivity tools (tasks, goals, habits) with mindfulness and self-care features (gratitude, affirmations, mood tracking, reflections). It's designed for people who want to stay organized *and* take care of their mental well-being — all in one book.

**What makes it different:**
- Most planners are *either* productivity-focused or wellness-focused. This does both.
- The S.A.R.E. tracker (Sleep, Appetite, Recreation, Energy) gives users a simple daily wellness snapshot — unique to this product.
- Customer-customizable accent color makes each book feel personal.
- Three layers of planning: Monthly overview → Weekly goals & habits → Daily tasks & reflections.

---

## 2. Target Audience

**Primary:** Young adults (18–35), especially:
- College students juggling classes, work, and self-care
- Young professionals building habits and routines
- Anyone starting a mindfulness or journaling practice

**Secondary:**
- Therapists/counselors who recommend journaling to clients
- Gift buyers looking for thoughtful, personal gifts
- Bullet journal enthusiasts who want pre-made structure

**Customer profile:**
> "I want to be more organized, but I also want to check in with myself. I don't want two separate books for that."

## 4. Manufacturing Options

### Option A: Print-on-Demand (POD)
**Best for:** Starting out, low risk, testing the market.

| Service | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Amazon KDP** | Largest marketplace, but limited binding options (no spiral) |
| **Lulu** | Supports spiral/wire-o, good print quality |
| **BookBaby** | Higher quality, more binding options, higher cost |

- **Pros:** No upfront inventory cost, prints as orders come in, easy to update designs.
- **Cons:** Higher per-unit cost ($5–10/book), less control over materials, limited custom colors.
- **Estimated unit cost:** $6–9 per book
- **Estimated retail price:** $18–25

### Option B: Bulk Printing
**Best for:** Once you've validated demand (50+ sales on POD).

| Printer Type | Notes |
|--------------|-------|
| **Domestic (US)** | Faster shipping, easier communication, higher cost |
| **Overseas (China/India)** | Lower cost, MOQ 200–500 units, longer lead time |
| **Alibaba suppliers** | Search "custom planner printing" — many specialize in this |

- **Pros:** Much lower per-unit cost ($2–4/book at 500+ units), full control over materials and colors.
- **Cons:** Upfront investment ($1,000–2,000 for first run), need storage/fulfillment.
- **Estimated unit cost:** $2.50–4 per book (at 500 units)
- **Estimated retail price:** $22–30 (higher perceived value)

### Recommendation
**Start with POD (Lulu or BookBaby)** to validate your product with real customers. Once you're consistently selling 50+/month, switch to bulk printing for better margins.

## 5. Pricing Strategy

| Channel | Price | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **POD (Lulu/Amazon)** | $19.99–$24.99 | Covers POD cost + platform fees |
| **Own website (Shopify/Etsy)** | $24.99–$29.99 | Higher margin, direct relationship |
| **Bulk-printed (own site)** | $24.99–$29.99 | Best margins ($20+ profit/book) |
| **Wholesale (to stores)** | $12–15 | 50% of retail, for boutiques/bookstores |

**Color customization premium:** Consider charging $2–3 extra for custom accent colors (when on bulk printing).

## 6. Sales Channels

### Phase 1 — Launch (Months 1–3)
1. **Etsy** — Perfect for handmade/unique planners. Low barrier to entry.
2. **Instagram / TikTok** — Visual product, perfect for short-form video content (flip-throughs, "plan with me" videos).
3. **Lulu / Amazon KDP** — POD fulfillment.

### Phase 2 — Growth (Months 4–8)
4. **Own website** (Shopify or similar) — Build your brand, collect emails, higher margins.
5. **Pinterest** — Planner audience is huge on Pinterest.
6. **Collaborations** — Partner with therapists, wellness influencers, or study content creators.

### Phase 3 — Scale (Months 9+)
7. **Wholesale** — Pitch to local bookstores, gift shops, wellness boutiques.
8. **Subscription model** — Quarterly planner delivery.
9. **Amazon FBA** — If demand justifies bulk inventory.
